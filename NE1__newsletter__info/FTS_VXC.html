Page 1 — The Science of Happiness

What really determines happiness? Modern research in positive psychology shows that happiness is influenced by three main components. The approximate breakdown commonly cited by researchers is:

  • ~50% Genetic set-point: Much of our baseline temperament and emotional reactivity is influenced by biology.
  • ~40% Intenciaj Agadoj (Intentional Activities): Deliberate practices and habits we choose to do regularly — this is where the largest gains are possible.
  • ~10% Life circumstances: Income, living conditions, and other external facts make a smaller contribution than most people expect.

Because nearly half of happiness is shaped by Intentional Activities, small, repeatable practices can raise well-being over time. Examples that research supports include:

  • Mindfulness meditation — improves attention, reduces stress, and increases emotional balance.
  • Regular exercise — releases mood-supporting chemicals and improves sleep and self-esteem.
  • Gratitude practices — noting things you appreciate fosters positive reappraisal.
  • Acts of kindness and social connection — deepen relationships and give purpose.
"Happiness is not a destination — it’s a way of traveling."

Happiness for students

Students face increasing stress and burnout. Learning how happiness works — and adopting small intentional activities — helps build resilience and reduces anxiety. This is a core reason the Fulmo Talk Series focuses on evidence-based well-being resources directed at college and university audiences.

Page 2 — About the Fulmo Talk Series

Inspiring ideas. Authentic voices. Global reach.

The Fulmo Talk Series is a collection of short, inspiring talks created especially for college and university students. Organized by the Esperanto and ConLang Society of South India, the series aims to make science-based, low-cost or free learning resources widely available — especially resources that support mental wellness and resilience.

What the series offers

  • Short, accessible talks on topics such as happiness, mental wellness, language innovation, disability rights, and eco-art.
  • Curated books and additional lectures on happiness and resilience research.
  • A focus on supporting students in India, Australia, the United States, and beyond.

2025 Highlights — Featured Speakers

  • Prof. Sonja Lyubomirsky — researcher on the science of happiness, practical strategies to increase well-being.
  • Prof. Sanjiv Chopra — Harvard physician and author, sharing ideas on purpose, gratitude, and health.
  • Anand Manikutty — technologist and inventor; language innovator introducing linguistic innovations and new concepts for learning.
  • Vishwanath Mallabadi — artist using e-waste to create eco-art and raise environmental awareness.

Mission & impact

The Fulmo Talk Series is run by a small non-profit initiative and presented by Fulmo Technologies. The project’s goals are to:

  • Share evidence-based tools for emotional well-being with students.
  • Encourage cross-cultural conversation and authentic thinking.
  • Help reduce the student mental health crisis by improving access to practical resources.

Fulmo's sister organization Qwykr Technologies has had their tools and technologies, including some of the technology behind Fulmo, recognized by startup programs such as Microsoft for Startups, Google for Startups, and the Wadhwani Foundation’s IGNITE program.
