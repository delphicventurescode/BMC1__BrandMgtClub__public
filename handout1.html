Welcome! This one-page handout explains how to prepare and run Demo Talks and Demo Sessions for the Fulmo Talk Series.

What is the Fulmo Talk Series?

Short, idea-driven talks (think TED-style) designed for clarity and broad appeal. Speakers present insights, stories, or practical ideas that anyone can understand.

Guidelines for Demo Talks

  • Translatable: Choose a topic that can be translated into Esperanto — i.e., clear, simple, and accessible.
  • Non-controversial: Topics must be of general interest; avoid narrow technical subjects or potentially divisive material.
  • No politics: Do not comment on political matters, especially Indian politics or politicians.
  • Length: Short Talk – 7–10 minutes. Long Talk – 15–18 minutes (18 min is a soft upper guideline).
  • Slides: Bring a PowerPoint deck. We can show you how to use ChatGPT to generate low-effort, effective slides.
  • Delivery: Be clear, engaging, and conversational — speak to a general audience.

Host Demo Sessions

Club members who understand the format can also host Demo Sessions — guiding other speakers through topic selection, slide tips, timing, and rehearsal. We already have a long list of external invitees who need Demo Sessions, so facilitators are essential.

How to volunteer

  • Sign up to present or to host at the sign-up sheet during today’s meeting.
  • If hosting, you’ll get a short checklist and a sample script to run a Demo Session.
  • We will offer a short workshop on using ChatGPT to create slides and speaker notes.

Quick facilitation tips: start on time, keep feedback constructive, focus on clarity of idea, and help the speaker stay within time limits.

At-a-glance

  • Short Talk: 7–10 min
  • Long Talk: 15–18 min
  • Slides: Speaker provides PPT
  • Topics: Translatable, non-controversial, general interest
  • Politics: No political content

Resources

We’ll provide: a Demo Session checklist, a speaker script template, and a short “Make slides with ChatGPT” guide.
